Quoting| What we need to get started

When you send uw an email to get a quote or start a print, you will need to send a .stl or .obj file. (other CAD files don't work for 3D Printing). If this seems confusing or is knew to know you can learn more about 3D Printing terminology from a post by ALL3d. This is a bit of a deep dive but is worth it to really understand what you are sending to us to print.

Attached to the message you send requesting a quote, you need the following info to begin a quote and/or get a print started.

  • What are the "Bounding Box" dimension of the part? (Round up not down)?
  • How does this part fit with your larger design or is it the complete design?
  • What is your goal for the print (show form or full function)?
  • What kind of loads or forces will this part be subjected to?
  • Does this part interface or interact with another parts (press-fit, snpa-fits, sliding joints)?
  • Do you have the native CAD file?
